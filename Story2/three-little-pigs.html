The Three Little Pigs

Once upon a time, there were three little pigs. Their names were Pip, Pop, and Pat.

One sunny day, Mama Pig said, “You are big now! It’s time to build your own houses.”

She gave each pig a hug and a kiss. Off they went!

Pip was the youngest. He wanted to play quickly.

“I will build my house with dry grass,” said Pip.

In one hour, his dry‑grass house was ready. Then he ran off to play.

Pop was the middle pig. He was a little careful.

“I will build my house with sticks,” said Pop.

Sticks were stronger than dry grass. Soon his stick house was ready. He also went to play.

Pat was the oldest. She was very careful.

“I will build my house with bricks,” said Pat.

She worked all day and all evening. At last, her brick house was strong and beautiful.

One dark night, a big bad wolf came. He was very hungry.

First, he went to Pip’s dry‑grass house.

“Little pig, let me come in!” growled the wolf.

“No way! I won’t let you in!” squeaked Pip.

“Then I will huff, and puff, and blow your house down!”

And he did! The dry‑grass house flew away. Pip ran to Pop’s stick house.

The wolf came to the stick house.

“Little pigs, let me come in!”

“No way! We won’t let you in!” shouted Pip and Pop.

The wolf huffed and puffed — and blew the stick house down!

Pip and Pop ran to Pat’s brick house.

The wolf came to the brick house.

“Little pigs, let me come in!” he roared.

“NO!” said all three pigs together.

The wolf huffed. He puffed. He huffed and puffed again.

But the brick house stayed strong. It did not move at all.

The wolf grew tired. He fell down, then walked away into the forest.

The three little pigs danced and cheered inside the brick house.

“Thank you, Pat!” said Pip and Pop.

Pat smiled. “Always build things properly, even if it takes more time.”

That night, the three little pigs slept safe and warm.


🌟 Moral of the Story:
Do things the right way, even if it takes more time and hard work. A strong foundation keeps you safe.
