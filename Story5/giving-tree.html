The Giving Tree

Once upon a time, there was a tall green tree.

She had big branches full of shiny green leaves.

And she loved one little boy very much.

Every day, the boy came to play.

He climbed her branches.

He swung from her arms.

He ate her sweet red apples.

He lay down in her cool shade and took naps.

The tree loved the boy.

And the boy loved the tree.

They were very happy.

As time passed, the boy grew older.

One day, he came back and said, “I want some toys. Can you give me money?”

“I have no money,” said the tree. “But you can take my apples and sell them.”

The boy took all the apples and went away.

The tree was happy.

Many years later, the boy came back again. He was grown up now.

“I want a house,” he said.

“I have no house,” said the tree. “But you can take my branches and build one.”

The boy cut the branches and went away.

The tree was happy.

More years passed. The boy came back once more. He was old now.

“I want a boat,” he said. “I want to sail far away.”

“Take my trunk,” said the tree softly. “Make a boat. That will make you happy.”

The boy cut down her trunk and sailed away.

The tree was happy — but not as happy as before.

Many, many years went by. One quiet evening, the boy came back again. He was very old and tired.

“I am sorry,” said the tree. “I have nothing left to give you. Only my old stump.”

The old man smiled. “I don’t need anything now. I just need a place to sit and rest.”

“Then sit,” said the tree. “Sit and rest.”

The old man sat down on the stump.

The tree was happy. And that was enough.

🌟 Moral of the Story:
The greatest love is selfless and giving.
It does not expect anything in return.
The greatest love gives without asking for anything in return.
Be thankful to those who care for you — just like trees give shade to everyone.
